Making Math Videos with Manim

When the world shutdown in early 2020, everyone suddenly had a bunch of free time on their hands. I decided to spend that time learning how use Grant Sanderson's (of 3blue1brown) manim library.

For those who don't know what manim is, it's essentially a tool that allows one to programmatically create a video. If you're interested in trying manim, I wrote a short article on how to get started.

Here's probably what's my favorite video I've made. It's an animation of the Lorenz Attractor.

Click to reveal my video on the Lorenz attractor.

Here's another cool strange attractor: the Aizawa Attractor.

Click to reveal my video on the Aizawa attractor.

I'm a little disappointed with where the manim project is headed. In 2020, the original manim project was forked into what is now the community edition (the community edition is named manim on pip). The community edition is meant to be better tested and more stable. However, the developers who maintain the community edition have made some questionable (to put it charitably) decisions. It seems that nowadays, the "community" is bent on enforcing their cringe code of conduct, rather than writing good code. Nevertheless, I do plan on making manim videos well into the future.
